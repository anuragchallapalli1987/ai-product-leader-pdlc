Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics

The foundational framework developed by Jakob Nielsen and Rolf Molich for identifying usability issues early in the design phase. A core competency for any NN/g-trained researcher.

#Heuristic PrincipleDescription
1Visibility of System StatusKeep users informed about system actions through appropriate feedback within reasonable time
2Match: System & Real WorldUse language, concepts, and metaphors familiar to the user rather than system-oriented terminology
3User Control & FreedomProvide "emergency exits" to undo actions and navigate freely without extended dialogues
4Consistency & StandardsAdhere to platform conventions; ensure consistent meaning for words and actions
5Error PreventionDesign to prevent errors from occurring; present confirmation options for critical actions
6Recognition over RecallMinimize memory load by making objects, actions, and instructions visible or easily retrievable
7Flexibility & EfficiencyCater to both novice and expert users with accelerators and customization options
8Aesthetic & Minimalist DesignRemove irrelevant information that competes with relevant units of information
9Error RecoveryExpress error messages in plain language that suggests solutions
10Help & DocumentationProvide searchable, task-focused documentation with concrete steps
Key Insight ‚Äî The Rule of 5
Testing with just 5 users can uncover approximately 85% of usability issues (NN/g). This enables lean, iterative testing cycles that provide rapid feedback, ensuring the product evolves in direct response to user needs ‚Äî without requiring large sample sizes.

Deloitte AI Governance Framework (DTG)

As firms increasingly use AI to generate deliverables, the QA Manager must implement structured governance to prevent reputational and regulatory risks. This includes defining AI-assisted work ownership, mandating human verification, and ensuring client-sensitive data is never used in AI prompts.

Critical Insight ‚Äî AI Governance
AI creates "lifecycle compression" ‚Äî developers produce code faster, but this places greater burden on QA to verify logic and security. The QA Manager's role has expanded from traditional testing to AI governance: owning structured checklists, audit trails, and compliance frameworks for all AI-generated deliverables.

Strategic Principles for AI Adoption

Five foundational principles that guide successful AI integration across the product development lifecycle. These principles ensure AI adoption enhances rather than conflicts with existing organizational responsibilities.

01

User-Focused, Not Tech-Driven

The AI strategy must support the core mission of solving customers' problems. AI tools should improve research, design, decision-making and build processes ‚Äî not chase novelty. This aligns with product-management best practice: leadership emphasizes delivering value and aligning cross-functional teams. AI for AI's sake creates technical debt and organizational confusion.

02

Start Simple, Scale Gradually

Anthropic's agentic-AI research shows that reliable AI systems are built by starting with simple tasks and adding complexity as confidence grows. Identify high-impact tasks (research synthesis, prototype generation, test-case generation) and pilot AI solutions there before expanding. Resist the temptation to deploy AI everywhere simultaneously.

03

Human-in-the-Loop for Quality

AI should augment staff, not replace them. Engineers at Anthropic reported that AI increased productivity but sometimes required extra time to debug mental-model mismatches. A human arbiter ensures AI outputs meet standards, particularly in design, strategic decisions and code. Automation without oversight creates invisible quality degradation.

04

Cross-Functional Governance

AI affects processes across product, design, development and testing. Establish governance (led by Product Management and UX Strategy) that sets standards on data privacy, acceptable uses, quality control and model alignment with organizational goals. Without governance, AI adoption fragments into inconsistent tribal practices.

05

Measure Everything, Trust Nothing

Track AI effectiveness with role-specific metrics: delivery speed, quality outcomes, team productivity, and employee satisfaction. Regularly review whether AI is delivering tangible business value. AI that "feels productive" but doesn't move metrics is organizational theater. Data-driven evaluation prevents sunk cost fallacy.
